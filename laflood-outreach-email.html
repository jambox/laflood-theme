Announcing LaFloodRecovery.org, a collection of resources for people to find and give help. 

September 1,  2016
New Orleans, LA
Contact: Info@FloordRecovery.org


LAFloodRecovery.org collects resources for people to find and give help in response to the August 2016 flooding in Louisiana and Mississippi

 

A team of eight web developers, disaster recovery specialists, and community organizers in New Orleans have created a comprehensive directory of resources for flood survivors and volunteers via the recently launched website, LAFloodRecovery.org. The site helps to connect volunteers, and donors, as well as those in need with organizations who are on the ground in Baton Rouge and Acadiana providing flood relief and recovery services.

With approximately 146,000 homes damaged by flooding and more than 100,000 individuals and households registered for assistance from the Federal Emergency Management Agency, this flooding is considered the worst natural disaster since Hurricane Sandy. Before the flood waters had receded in Baton Rouge, hundreds of relief organizations sprung into action in Louisiana and the affected areas of Mississippi.

Eli Silverman, a web developer behind the project, saw that the thousands of individuals across the country who were seeking to volunteer their time, goods, and money found it difficult to actually take action. "There was an incredible amount of noise and redundant or vague information being passed around on the web, so I thought the best impact I could have would be to use my design and coding skills to organize that information for both volunteers and those looking for relief."

Over the past two weeks, the team has cataloged and categorized hundreds of relief resources on LAFloodRecovery.org, from FEMA disaster assistance documents to Church group locations distributing food and goods on the ground. The team aims to keep the site up-to-date and comprehensive by inviting the public to use the quick submission form to add organizations and available services that are missing from the initial list.

We would appreciate your support by sharing this information with people still searching for flood relief resources and with organizations that should have their services listed on LaFloodRecovery.org.
